Municipal Incentives & Development Charges

Active fiscal incentives creating a time-sensitive development window. Critical deadline: August 31, 2027.

Per-Lot Development Charge Savings
$19,702
20% reduction on standard rate of $98,511 per single-detached unit — discounted to $78,809
Expires August 31, 2027

Development Charge Reduction Program

Detail Value
Program DurationSeptember 1, 2025 – August 31, 2027
Reduction20% across all residential development
Standard Rate$98,511 per single-detached unit
Discounted Rate$78,809 per single-detached unit
Savings Per Lot$19,702
RationaleHamilton Council pilot to stimulate housing supply; expected $9.6M annual cost to city

Energy Efficiency Rebates

Provincial Home Renovation Savings Program — available for new construction:

Geothermal
$2,000 – $12,000 rebate. Ideal for estate lots with space for ground loops. Suited to private well/septic properties.
Heat Pump
$500 – $7,500 rebate. Cold climate air-source heat pumps — efficient year-round heating and cooling.
Solar PV
$1,000 per kW (typical $3,000–$5,000 total). Excellent ROI for luxury builds with energy strategies.

Secondary Dwelling Units (SDUs)

SDU Opportunity

Up to 2 SDUs permitted per single-detached lot (By-law 05-200). 100% Development Charge exemption for SDUs. Potential Housing Accelerator Fund support: permit fee rebate up to $2,000/unit; forgivable loan up to $25,000/unit (if rented at/below AMR for 15 years).

Verification Required

SDU eligibility in Greensville's rural boundaries (HHCIPA) requires verification with the City prior to marketing claims.

Section 11

Development Strategy & Recommendations

Immediate actions and design benchmarks to capitalize on the DC incentive window before August 31, 2027.

19-Month Window

Each month of delay = lost savings opportunity. File permits before August 31, 2027 to lock in $19,702 per-unit savings before standard rates (~$98,000) reinstate.

Design Benchmarks

  • 4+ bedrooms minimum (align with 37% current standard)
  • 2,500–3,500+ sq ft (estate lot justification)
  • 2–3 full bathrooms (upscale standard)
  • Open-concept, high ceilings (address pre-1960 limitations)
  • Smart home automation & EV charging

Sustainable Systems

  • Geothermal heating ($2K–$12K rebate)
  • Air-source heat pumps ($500–$7.5K rebate)
  • Solar PV ($1K/kW; ~$3K–$5K total)
  • Net-zero capability positioning
  • Premium finishes, minimal operational costs

SDU Opportunity

For estate lots with 1+ acre — build primary residence (4+ bedrooms) and add garden suite or laneway house (SDU, 100% DC exempt). Market as:

Multi-Generational
Aging parent suite for the 22% senior population
Professional Studio
Home office or creative workspace separate from main home
Income Suite
Up to $25K forgivable loan if rented at/below AMR for 15 years

Risk Mitigation

Market and regulatory risks with corresponding mitigation strategies.

RiskDetailMitigation
Inventory Expansion Hamilton listings +7.1% in 2025; market moving toward buyer-favourable Emphasize Greensville's permanent scarcity (conservation boundaries, single-detached mandate)

  • Septic/Well Systems: Private servicing requirements mean larger lot minimums; verify soil conditions and water table per parcel

  • SDU Regulations: SDU regs and DC exemptions should be confirmed with City before marketing "multi-generational" features
  • Build Timeline: 18-month design-to-permit window is tight for custom luxury builds; engage architect/engineer early
